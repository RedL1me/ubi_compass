# Startup Ecosystem

A startup ecosystem is an interconnected network of individuals, organizations, resources, and support structures that collectively foster the creation, growth, and success of startups within a particular region or industry. It includes startups at various stages, investors, educational institutions, support organizations, corporates, government bodies, and service providers working together to promote innovation and entrepreneurship.
## Did You Know
Startup ecosystems operate through symbiotic relationships among entrepreneurs, mentors, investors, incubators, accelerators, universities, and government agencies. These stakeholders collaborate by sharing knowledge, capital, talent, and infrastructure. Effective ecosystems provide access to funding, market opportunities, mentorship, and a supportive community, accelerating startup development and scaling.
## Helpful Information
Thriving startup ecosystems balance both “hard” infrastructure (e.g., physical spaces and funding) and “soft” infrastructure (e.g., community culture and knowledge sharing). Ecosystem strength is often measured by startup survival rates, funding activity, talent availability, and innovation output. Incubators and accelerators serve as critical nexus points, facilitating connections and resources among ecosystem participants.
