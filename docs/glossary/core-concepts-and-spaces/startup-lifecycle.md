# Startup Lifecycle

The startup lifecycle encompasses the key stages a startup progresses through from inception to maturity. These stages typically include ideation, validation, product-market fit, scaling, maturity, and potential exit. Each phase reflects shifts in business objectives, operational complexity, resource needs, and external engagement.
## Did You Know
Startups begin with the ideation and discovery phases, validating ideas through customer research and prototypes. Following early validation, they secure funding and develop scalable business models at the growth or scaling stage. Mature startups focus on optimizing operations, expanding markets, and possibly preparing for exits such as acquisitions or IPOs. Support from incubators, accelerators, investors, and ecosystem partners is pivotal at each stage, tailored to the evolving needs.
## Bonus Fact
The lifecycle stages provide a roadmap for targeted interventions, increasing startup success rates and ecosystem maturity. Lifecycle models may vary, but share common phases critical for navigating startup uncertainty and growth.
