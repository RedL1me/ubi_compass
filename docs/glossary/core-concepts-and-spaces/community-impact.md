# Community Impact

## Metric Definition
Assessment of social initiatives’ outputs and outcomes within the community.
Definition
Evaluates concrete social benefits generated by the program’s community engagement efforts.
Purpose
To measure the program’s contribution to social well-being and cohesion in its ecosystem.
Rationale
Social impact is a key facet of ESG and critical for sustainable ecosystem development.
Advantages
Limitations
Captures tangible benefits beyond economic metrics.
Private/corporate programs may have less focus on social mandates.
Enhances program reputation and stakeholder buy-in.
Measurement and attribution remain challenging.
Climate Strategy
DEI Monitoring
