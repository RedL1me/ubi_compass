# Joint Venture

A joint venture (JV) is a business arrangement where two or more organizations pool resources to accomplish a specific, mutually agreed-upon goal. Each participant maintains its independence and shares in the profits, losses, and management as outlined in their agreement. JVs are typically formed for particular projects or ventures with a defined scope and duration.
### Did You Know
Participants in a joint venture contribute assets, expertise, and resources to a new or existing project, such as entering new markets, developing new products, or sharing technology. Control, profit sharing, and risk are governed by a formal contract tailored to the scope of the project. JVs often combine the strengths of participating companies to reduce risk and increase chances of success in high-stakes, high-reward initiatives.
### Bonus Fact
Pursue a joint venture to accelerate innovation efforts or speed up product development and commercialization that would be slower or more costly independently.
