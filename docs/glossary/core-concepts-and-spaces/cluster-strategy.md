# Cluster Strategy

A cluster strategy is a place-based economic approach in which startups, academic institutions, corporations, and related organizations in a specific industry and geographic area collaborate to achieve shared growth and competitiveness. These structured networks leverage local resources, talent, and infrastructure, facilitating the exchange of knowledge and fostering innovation in a defined sector or region.
## Did you know...
This strategy taps into the unique strengths of a particular region or sector, encouraging participants to pool resources, address common challenges, and co-create innovative solutions. By forming trusted partnerships and concentrating expertise, cluster strategies boost productivity, attract new investment, and accelerate both job creation and advancement of new technologies. Examples include tech clusters in Silicon Valley, automotive hubs in Detroit, or life sciences networks in Boston.
Bonus knowledge: Effective clusters are marked by diversity among participants, robust support infrastructure, and a shared commitment to continual innovation.
