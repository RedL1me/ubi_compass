# Business Accelerator

A fixed-term, cohort-based organization focused on later-stage startups, concentrating on rapid scaling, investment-readiness, and market traction. Accelerators are typically intense, lasting from a few weeks up to six months, and culminate in a public pitch or demo day. Source: UBI Global
A program that applies a similar set of services and resources to existing companies, rather than startups, with the specific goal of helping these small businesses grow more rapidly (accelerate). Source: InBIA (International Business Innovation Association)
A program providing intensive business support, such as coaching and mentoring, to a cohort of startups for a limited period. These programs are often linked to seed funding in exchange for equity and are highly selective. Source: The European Commission
A model distinguished by its fixed-term, cohort-based structure, competitive entry, and provision of pre-seed or seed investment, often in exchange for equity. The process is intense, compressed, and culminates in a graduation or "demo day" event. Source: General Academic Consensus
## Did you know...
Accelerators engage startups through a structured curriculum of mentorship, networking, business development, and funding opportunities. Founders work closely with experienced entrepreneurs, industry experts, and investors to refine their business models and scale operations quickly. Programs foster valuable peer networks within cohorts and provide access to capital, workspace, and sector-specific resources that collectively enhance growth potential.
