# Mentor

Mentors are seasoned professionals who guide startup founders by sharing deep industry knowledge, wisdom, and experience. Unlike coaches who work on shorter timelines and focus on structured support, mentors build long-term relationships with entrepreneurs, advising and supporting them throughout the life of their business.
## Did You Know
Mentors direct, teach, and advise founders by providing strategic insights, opening networks, and helping navigate challenges. They act as trusted advisors who know when to listen, when to encourage, and when to challenge assumptions. Effective mentorship includes both business guidance and personal support, fostering resilience and leadership development over time.
## Helpful Information
UBI Global research shows that startups with mentors have higher survival rates and accelerated growth. Successful mentoring relies on a strong personal connection, relevant expertise, and open communication. Mentors often help founders gain confidence, avoid common pitfalls, and scale sustainably, making them invaluable partners in the startup journey.
## Advisor, Coach or Mentor?
Mentors develop long-term, holistic relationships focused on growth; coaches facilitate structured, performance-driven improvement; and advisors offer specialized, issue-focused guidance. Many startups benefit from engaging all three types at different stages and needs of the venture.
