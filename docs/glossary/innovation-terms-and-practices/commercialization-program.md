# Commercialization Program

Commercialization programs are organization- or corporate-driven initiatives that support research teams and startups in bringing new technologies and innovations to market. These programs facilitate licensing agreements, pilot partnerships, and first-customer sales, often without requiring startups to surrender equity or intellectual property rights, distinguishing them from traditional startup accelerators.
### Did You Know
These programs provide tailored resources such as industry expertise, legal support, market validation, and access to corporate customers. They help innovators navigate commercialization pathways by connecting them with strategic partners and facilitating real-world testing opportunities. Unlike startup accelerators, commercialization programs focus less on equity investment and more on enabling practical adoption and scale of new technologies.
### Bonus Fact
Commercialization programs are critical in bridging the gap between invention and market readiness, especially for deep-tech innovations. By reducing barriers like IP concerns and investment pressures, these programs encourage broader participation and faster market adoption. Corporations and research institutions frequently partner with commercialization programs to accelerate innovation impact and economic growth.
