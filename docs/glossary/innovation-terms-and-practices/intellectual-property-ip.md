# Intellectual Property (IP)

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to intangible creations of the human mind, such as inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, symbols, names, and images used in commerce. IP grants exclusive rights to creators and innovators to control and benefit from the use of their creations, fostering innovation, creativity, and economic value.
## Did You Know
IP rights include patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, and industrial designs. These rights enable individuals and companies to protect innovations against unauthorized use, secure competitive advantages, and attract investment. Managing IP strategically is critical for commercialization, technology transfer, and sustaining business growth across industries.
## Bonus Fact
Effective IP management encourages innovation by ensuring inventors and creators are rewarded for their efforts. It plays a vital role in research commercialization, technology licensing, and building brand equity. Intellectual property rights vary by jurisdiction but universally aim to balance incentives for innovation with public access to knowledge and cultural goods.
## IP and AI: A New Era
AI is reshaping IP by challenging concepts of creativity, inventorship, and ownership, driving rapid legal adaptation to ensure fair protection, encourage innovation, and manage risks in the AI era. Incubators, accelerators, and their network of partners should be aware of recent changes in their jurisdictions surrounding IP and AI.
