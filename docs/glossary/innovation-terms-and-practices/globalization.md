# Globalization

Globalization in the startup context refers to the widespread diffusion of innovation beyond traditional hubs, with new cities and countries emerging as vital global centers for startups and investors. Startup incubators and accelerators are purposefully designed to help startups access, navigate, and scale within international markets by structuring internal teams, resources, and networks to foster global growth and cross-border collaboration.
## Did You Know
Globalization involves building international partnerships, integrating global mentors, and creating networks that span countries and continents. Incubators and accelerators tailor their programs to equip startups with the skills, connections, and market insights necessary to compete globally. This worldwide connectivity enables startups to tap into diverse markets, access cross-border funding, and collaborate across cultures.
## Bonus Fact
Globalization fuels innovation acceleration by facilitating knowledge exchange and resource sharing worldwide. Growing startup hubs such as San Francisco, New York, London, Beijing, and Bangalore exemplify this trend, each contributing unique strengths to the global innovation landscape. Startups that effectively utilize global networks and resources gain competitive advantages and unlock new growth opportunities in diverse markets.
