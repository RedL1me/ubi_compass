# Venture Builder (Startup Studio)

Organizations that systematically produce new ventures by providing internal funding, resources, and cross-functional teams. Venture builders ideate, launch, and support startups from within, often having equity in each.
### Did you know...
Venture builders engage by assembling dedicated teams that bring expertise in product development, business strategy, marketing, and technology. They apply methodologies such as Lean Startup, Agile development, and design thinking to rapidly build minimum viable products (MVPs), test market fit, and iterate toward scalable business models. Throughout the journey, venture builders provide capital, shared services, talent acquisition, and operational support, helping ventures overcome typical startup challenges with a hands-on approach.
## Related Definitions
- Startup Studio: Another term for venture builder; an organization that systematically creates startups.
- Corporate Venture Building: Corporations adopting the venture builder model to innovate and spin off new businesses.
- Venture Capital: Investment firms that provide funding but typically do not operate or manage startups directly.
- Sweat Equity: Non-financial contributions such as labor and expertise that earn equity in a venture.
### Bonus Fact
This approach is particularly well-suited for ambitious projects that require close collaboration and continuous adaptation to market demands.
