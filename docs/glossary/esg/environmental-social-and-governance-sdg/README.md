# Environmental, Social & Governance (SDG)

ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance, representing a set of standards used to measure a company's impact on the planet, society, and how its leadership governs operations. These criteria evaluate how organizations manage environmental risks, social responsibilities, and corporate governance to promote ethical, sustainable, and transparent business practices.
## Did You Know
ESG encompasses a broad range of practices, including reducing carbon emissions, ensuring workplace diversity and inclusion, protecting human rights within the supply chain, and maintaining transparent leadership structures. Companies use ESG metrics to attract socially conscious investors, improve risk management, and align with regulatory requirements. ESG performance is increasingly integrated into corporate strategy and investor decision-making processes.
## Related Definitions
- Sustainability: Meeting present needs without compromising future generations’ ability to meet theirs.
- Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR): Voluntary business efforts to contribute positively to society and the environment.
- Impact Investing: Investments aimed at generating positive social or environmental impact alongside financial returns.
- Stakeholder Engagement: Involving stakeholders in decision-making to ensure responsible and inclusive business outcomes.
## The UBI Global Network
In the last UBI Global World Benchmark Study for 2021-2022, the following ESG-related facts were revealed by the research.
- 74% of the incubators and accelerators in the Study engage with ESG. The organizations in the Study represented $4bn+ investments in ESG and 9500+ community impact jobs during the Study period.
- Higher ESG Engagement scores are associated with higher Program Impact & Performance Scores (PIPS) in the main benchmark.
- Higher ESG Engagement is NOT associated with reduced returns – for programs or their client startups.
