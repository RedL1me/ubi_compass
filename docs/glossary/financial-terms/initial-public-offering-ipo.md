# Initial Public Offering (IPO)

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process by which a privately held company offers shares of its stock to the public for the first time. This transition from private to public ownership enables the company to raise significant capital, enhance market visibility, and provide liquidity for early investors.
## Did You Know
The IPO process involves preparing detailed disclosures and financial documents for regulatory approval, commonly led by investment banks acting as underwriters. Companies typically conduct roadshows to attract institutional and individual investors. Post-IPO, shares are traded on public stock exchanges, establishing market-driven pricing and ownership diversification.
## Bonus Fact
Incubators often prepare companies for IPO readiness through governance, financial strategy, and investor relations coaching.
