# Exit Strategy

An exit strategy is a deliberate plan developed by entrepreneurs, business owners, or investors to sell their ownership stake or transfer control of a company once certain business milestones or financial objectives are achieved. It provides a pathway to realize returns on investment, minimize losses, or transition leadership while preserving company value.
## Did You Know
Exit strategies guide critical decisions on timing, valuation, and transition processes. Common exit paths include initial public offerings (IPOs), mergers and acquisitions (M&A), management buyouts, or liquidation. Successful execution requires planning, evaluation of market conditions, and alignment among stakeholders to optimize outcomes and ensure smooth exit transitions.
## Bonus Fact
Developing an exit strategy early in the startup lifecycle helps founders and investors align on long-term goals and prepare for future transitions. A clear exit plan can attract investment by demonstrating foresight and risk management. It is also crucial for managing personal and financial expectations and facilitating legacy building or reinvestment opportunities.
