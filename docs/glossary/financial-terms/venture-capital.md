# Venture Capital

Venture capital (VC) is a form of private equity financing provided to startup companies and small businesses with high growth potential. VC firms invest capital in exchange for equity, supporting companies that often lack access to traditional funding due to high risk and limited operating history.
### Did You Know
Venture capitalists not only provide funding but also strategic guidance, managerial expertise, and networking opportunities. They typically invest with a long-term outlook, aiming for substantial returns through company growth, acquisition, or public offering. VC involvement often includes active participation on company boards and collaboration with founders to accelerate business development.
### Bonus Fact
Incubators and accelerators often work closely with VC firms to prepare startups for investment readiness and scaling challenges.
