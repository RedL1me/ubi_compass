# Angel Investor or Angel Group

Angel investors are typically high-net-worth individuals who provide seed capital and mentorship to startups at very early stages. Angel groups are collectives of such investors pooling resources and expertise to invest in promising startups, often preceding or complementing venture capital funding rounds. Investment amounts are generally smaller than those of venture capitalists and come in exchange for equity or convertible debt.
Did You Know
Angel investors seek startups with innovative ideas and strong growth potential, often participating actively by providing advice, industry connections, and operational support. They play a critical role in bridging the funding gap between founders’ personal resources and institutional investments. Angel groups coordinate deal sourcing, due diligence, and portfolio management to optimize investment impact and support startups effectively.
Bonus Fact
Incubators and accelerators frequently facilitate connections between startups and angel investors, enhancing investment readiness and ecosystem growth.
